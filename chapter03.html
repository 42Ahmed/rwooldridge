NoteFrisch-Waugh-Lovell Theorem

In a multiple regression, the coefficient for \(x_1\) represents partial effect of \(x_1\) on \(y\) after removing overlop with other variables:

  1. Regress: \(x_1 = \alpha_0 + \alpha_1 x_2 + \alpha_2 x_3 + u\)
  2. Extract: \(\hat{u}\) (This is “\(x_1\)” that is completely uncorrelated with \(x_2\) and \(x_3\)).
  3. Result: The slope \(\beta_1\) from \(y = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \hat{u} + e\) is identical to the coefficient in the full multiple regression.
  4. The Formula\[\hat{\beta}_{x_1} = \frac{\sum \hat{u}_i y_i}{\sum \hat{u}_i^2}\]
